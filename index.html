So what's the point of this virtual simulation?


In this simulation, you'll notice a few things.
  1. 1. You can manipulate the temperature of the gas
  2. 2. You can manipulate the molar mass of the gas
  3. 3. The gas response in response to this!

The average kinetic energy for a gas is calculated via 3/2(R*n)*T, where R is the rate constant for a gas, n is the number of moles, and T is the temperature. So one might ask, where does mass come into this? Well, this is only kinetic energy. When it comes to exploring the velocity of a gas, we must take another equation into consideration. Ek=1/2mu^2, where m is mass, and u is the mean sqaure speed. By isolating u, we then come to a problem; what is the mass? Well, we can use our molar mass for this purpose. This is why molar mass affects the velocity of a gas. But, why doesn't it affect pressure? Well, you see, pressure is calculated via the ideal gas law. The ideal gas law doesn't take molar mass into consideration.
What this demonstrates to us is one of the key weaknesses of the ideal gas law, the fact that it doesn't take molar mass into conisderation, when clearly the change of molar mass causes more/less collisions with the container according to the simulation, which would affect pressure
